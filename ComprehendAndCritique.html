Comprehending and critiqueing

I am using the socratic seminars to demonstrate my ability to comprehend and critique. In my first socratic seminar I had to read an article on southernization, then make a map with locations, bodys of water, sources of important resources and trade routs. Then in class we had a discussion on what southernization was and who it influenced. On this socratic seminar I got a lower grade because I did not speak very much. My second socratic seminar was about exploitation and how masculinity affects Okonkwo. On this socratic seminar I got an A because I spoke a lot and voiced my opinions. My growth is demonstrated here because as I practiced doing socratic seminars I got better. In my second seminar I was able to listen to what my classmates said and thought and then add onto their ideas or counter them with ideas of my own. Currently, I give myself an A in this area because I have grown my ability to comprehend and critique: I can be engaged and open minded in my discussion, I asses if my classmates are saying something that is true or not, and I question what different people say. My goal for next semester is to keep comprehending and critiquing in assignments such as the socratic seminar because I would like to continue to get high grades in my classes.
