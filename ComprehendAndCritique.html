Comprehending and Critiquing

I am using the Socratic Seminars to demonstrate my ability to comprehend and critique. In my first Socratic Seminar I had to read an article on southernization, then make a map with locations, bodies of water, sources of important resources and trade rouets. Then in class we had a discussion on what southernization was and who it influenced. On this Socratic Seminar I got a lower grade because I did not speak very much. My second Socratic Seminar was about exploitation and how masculinity affects Okonkwo in Things Fall Apart. On this Socratic Seminar I got an A because I spoke a lot and voiced my opinions. My growth is demonstrated here because as I practiced doing Socratic Seminars I got better. In my second seminar I was able to listen to what my classmates said and thought and then add onto their ideas or counter them with ideas of my own. Currently, I give myself an A in this area because I have grown my ability to comprehend and critique: I can be engaged and open minded in my discussion, I assess if my classmates are saying something that is true or not, and I question what different people say. My goal for next semester is to keep comprehending and critiquing in assignments such as the Socratic Seminar because I would like to continue to improve my skills in this area.
